POHA

Poha is a light and flavorful Indian breakfast dish made from flattened rice, spices, and fresh vegetables.

It is especially popular in states like Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh and is loved for its simplicity and quick preparation

This dish is mildly spiced, slightly tangy, and often garnished with fresh coriander, lemon juice, and crunchy peanuts

poha is not only delicious but also easy to digest, making it a perfect start to your day.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups thick poha (flattened rice
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 small potato (finely chopped, optional)
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 8–10 curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons peanuts
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

STEPS

  1. Rinse the poha in water once or twice and drain it completely. Let it sit for 5 minutes to soften.

  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.

  3. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter

  4. Add peanuts and roast until golden brown

  5. Add curry leaves and green chilies; sauté for a few seconds.

  6. Add chopped onions (and potatoes if using) and cook until soft.

  7. Add turmeric powder and mix well.

  8. Add the soaked poha, salt, and sugar. Mix gently so the poha doesn’t break.

  9. Cook for 2–3 minutes on low flame.

  10. Turn off the heat and add lemon juice.

  11. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.
